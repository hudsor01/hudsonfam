servings: "12 biscuits"

ingredients:
  - "2 cups all-purpose flour"
  - "1 tablespoon baking powder"
  - "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
  - "1 teaspoon salt"
  - "6 tablespoons cold butter, cubed"
  - "3/4 cup cold buttermilk"
instructions:
  - "Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment."
  - "Whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl."
  - "Cut in the cold butter with a pastry blender until the mixture looks like coarse crumbs with a few pea-sized pieces."
  - "Pour in the buttermilk and stir just until a shaggy dough forms. Do not overmix."
  - "Turn the dough out onto a floured surface, pat to 3/4-inch thick, and fold over on itself two or three times."
  - "Cut rounds with a 2-inch cutter, pressing straight down without twisting. Place on the baking sheet so the biscuits touch."
  - "Bake 10–12 minutes until golden and tall. Brush the tops with melted butter while warm."

This one is the cornerstone of every Hudson holiday breakfast. Grandma never
measured the buttermilk exactly — she'd say to add it "until the dough tells you
to stop." The amount above is our best modern translation of her handful-and-a-splash
method.

> The secret, she always said, is keeping everything cold and your hands quick.
